01 THE MISSION

Exploring how black holes consume matter

NEBULA-Xplorer is a student-led Dutch satellite mission studying X-ray binaries to understand matter near black holes. The project combines research on extreme gravity with hands-on satellite construction. Launch for NEBULA-Xplorer is planned for 2030.

  • SCIENCE
    What we study

    NEBULA-Xplorer investigates X-ray binaries, including systems with black holes and neutron stars. By observing how matter behaves in these extreme environments, the mission helps researchers better understand accretion processes and cosmic variability.

  • TECHNOLOGY
    How we observe

    The mission uses a compact satellite with X-ray optics and detectors to capture high-energy signals from space. Its design allows for long-duration observations, helping scientists collect data over time instead of only in short moments.

  • OIMPACT
    Why it matters

    NEBULA-Xplorer is not only a research mission, but also an educational one. Students, researchers, and industry partners work together to design, build, and test a real satellite, creating both scientific knowledge and future talent for the Dutch space sector.

02THE SATELLITE

Inside the NEBULA-Xplorer spacecraft

The NEBULA-Xplorer spacecraft is a compact scientific satellite designed to observe high-energy X-ray radiation from extreme cosmic environments. It combines a specialized instrument with multiple supporting systems that enable it to operate in space, collect data, and communicate with Earth. Each component on board has a specific function, from generating power and maintaining stability to capturing and processing scientific data. Together, these systems form a fully integrated platform designed for precise and long-duration observations.
